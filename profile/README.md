## Who We Are  
At LibreTech Collective, we believe technology should be open, transparent, and accessible to all. Whether you're a coder, designer, tinkerer, hacker - or simply curious about free technology - you belong here with us!  

All Georgia Tech students, faculty, and alumni are welcome to join. There's never any membership dues or gatekeeping at LibreTech Collective - just pure passion and curiosity!

## Our Mission  
We aim to:
- Build hands-on free projects - **both software and hardware** - from the ground up.  
- Contribute upstream to major free/libre software projects.
- Host hackathons, code sprints, workshops, and speaker events.  
- Advocate for user freedom, ethical technology practices, and awareness of digital rights on campus.  
- Foster a community of learning and teaching - where everyone grows together.  

* LibreTech Collective is fully independent and student-run, with all established Georgia Tech policies respected. Our mission comes from our members, never the institute.*

## What Is Free (Libre) Software?  
Free software does not always “free of cost” - instead, is defined by the respect it affords to users: the freedom to **Run**, **Study**, **Modify**, and **Share**. These are known as the "Four Essential Freedoms" articulated by the Free Software Foundation.

### Our Licensing Policy  
- ✅ **Allowed (and preferred):** GPL-Compatible Copyleft licenses (GPLv3, LGPLv3, MPL 2.0, etc.)  
- ✅ **Allowed:** GPL-Compatible Permissive licenses (BSD, MIT, Apache 2.0).
- ❌ **Forbidden:** Source-available-only licenses (BSL, SSPL, etc.)  

>[!IMPORTANT]
>The only requirement is that whatever license you choose, the project’s source code must remain publicly viewable, modifiable, and redistributable. We will never accept “source-available” licenses - these do not meaningfully protect user freedoms, and are effectively no better than most proprietary licenses.

## Why It Matters
We’re more than a programming club - we’re a movement grounded in computing freedom, ethical collaboration, and shared knowledge. We see ourselves as a blend of hacktivists and students, united by a belief in free technology. Whether you’re building, contributing, or learning with us, you’re helping create a space where technology empowers rather than controls.
